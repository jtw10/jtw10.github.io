Why Watch T.V.?

Allow me to introduce you to some great television series. Do you ever feel lethargic and need a break?
A great way to give yourself a break is to grab a snack and indulge in some television series.

Here is an explanation on why people watch T.V. While some people see T.V. as a negative thing, there are actually benefits to allowing yourself to briefly escape into another world. Remember to enjoy yourself as long as you use your television in moderation! Don't forget to get up, stretch, and go outside.

Gotham

Gotham is an American crime drama television series developed by Bruno Heller and based on characters published by DC Comics and appearing in the Batman franchise, primarily those of James Gordon and Bruce Wayne. Danny Cannon directed the pilot, and he is an executive producer along with Heller. The series stars Ben McKenzie as the young James Gordon. Gotham was filming in Vancouver outside the Vancouver Art Gallery as recently as March 2019. Gotham is an entertaining show for fans of the Batman franchise and the thrill of each episode keeps viewers on the edge of their seats.

Here is a trailer for Gotham Season 5.

Arrow

Arrow is an American superhero television series developed by writer/producers Greg Berlanti, Marc Guggenheim, and Andrew Kreisberg that is based on the DC Comics character Green Arrow. The series premiered in the United States on The CW on October 10, 2012, with international broadcasting taking place in late 2012. Primarily filmed in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada, Arrow follows billionaire playboy Oliver Queen (Stephen Amell), who, five years after being stranded on a hostile island, returns home to fight crime and corruption as a secret vigilante whose weapon of choice is a bow and arrow. Arrow is perfect for fans of superhero shows.

As a native Vancouverite, there is extra satisfaction when I am able to spot scenes in the series that I can recognize and locate.

The Producers

The Producers is a realistic yet fictional drama about the events at a South Korean public T.V. station. A group of young television producers - Ra Joon Mo (Cha Tae Hyun), Baek Seung Chan (Kim Soo Hyun), Tak Ye Jin (Gong Hyo Jin) - and K-pop star Cindy (IU) work tirelessly around the clock within the entertainment division of a major broadcasting network. It is unique and different from any other drama, with tiny plot twists and comedy. The character development over time was wonderful.

Here is a trailer for The Producers.

Dream High

Fresh, fun and earnest, and backed by a breezy soundtrack that’s easy on the ears, Dream High ranks as one of my all-time favorite music-centric, high school dramas. It’s undoubtedly one of my favorite dramas. Despite its youthful premise, Dream High has an universal appeal with its emphasis on friendship, loyalty, and dedication to pursuing your dreams. What the idol-heavy cast lacks in finesse, they more than make up for with earnestness. And then there’s Kim Soo Hyun, Uhm Ki Joon and Lee Yoon Ji in the main cast, lending acting cred and nuance to the overall package.
